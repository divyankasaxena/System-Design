In a microservices environment, service instances are constantly created, destroyed, and restarted. Their IP addresses change frequently.

Hardcoding service addresses is therefore impractical.

Service Discovery allows services to locate each other dynamically.

Each service registers itself with a Service Registry when it starts.

Other services query the registry to find healthy instances.

To ensure only active services receive requests, each service periodically sends a Heartbeat.

If heartbeats stop, the registry removes that instance from the available list.

Two common discovery models are:

Client-Side Discovery

The client contacts the Service Registry, selects a healthy instance, and communicates directly with it.

Advantages:

Flexible
Client controls load balancing

Disadvantages:

Discovery logic must exist in every client.
Server-Side Discovery

The client sends requests to a Load Balancer.

The Load Balancer queries the Service Registry and forwards requests to healthy service instances.

Advantages:

Simpler clients
Centralized routing

Distributed Coordination helps multiple services work safely together.

Common coordination tasks include:

Leader Election
Distributed Locking
Configuration Management
Cluster Membership
Failover Handling

Popular coordination tools include:

ZooKeeper
etcd
Consul

Key Points

Service Discovery eliminates hardcoded service addresses.
Services register themselves automatically.
Heartbeats detect failed instances.
Distributed Coordination ensures services cooperate safely.
